Case Studies

Shizuka is the app for people with anxiety. Outfitted with several different tools chosen for people looking to manage anxiety and anxiety attacks—including a mood and thought tracker, breathing exercises, and a curated list of distracting items—and carefully researched to ensure everything about the app is as calming and helpful as possible, Shizuka is designed to be a one-stop experience for anxious individuals. Click here to learn more.

KitchKit is a hypothetical subscription box that sends interested users a box full of kitchen tools, dry ingredients, and recipes from a different country every month. The website is designed to entice users to order and to make checking out easy to reduce the risk of customer drop-off. Click here to learn more.

Lunch Break is an iOS app that allows users to find people nearby with whom they can enjoy lunch and a conversation. Inspired by my move to a new area. this app is intended especially for people who are new to an area, find it difficult to ask people to eat with them, or just want to find new friends. Click here to learn more.

Corner is an online SAAS for users who want a convenient place to save, organize, and share images, text, and links. It’s especially useful for designers and teams who need a communal space in which they can share inspiration and notes. Click here to learn more.

about

I am a UX designer and researcher located in the San Francisco Bay Area. My specialty is well-researched, clean designs, and my passion is making these designs easy to use for everyone. As a graduate from BYU’s psychology program, I understand the importance of human factors in building great websites and apps, and as someone with experience in applied research both in and outside of UX as well as in design, I know how to embrace those factors to make things go from good to brilliant.

Also, I’m just a charming, hilarious person*, so there’s that too.
